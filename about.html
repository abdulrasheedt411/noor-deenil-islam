Understanding Islam

The Message of Peace, Justice, and Pure Monotheism (Tawheed). Islam as revealed in the Quran and taught by Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

What is Islam?

Islam is not a new religion but the same eternal message revealed through all prophets including Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and finally Muhammad (peace be upon them all). The word "Islam" means submission to the will of Allah and comes from the same root as "Salaam" - meaning peace.

Islam teaches that all human beings are born pure and are accountable directly to Allah. It provides a complete way of life covering spiritual, social, economic, and political aspects based on the Quran and the example of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

"Indeed, the religion in the sight of Allah is Islam." - Quran 3:19

Quick Facts About Islam

  • Followers: 1.9+ billion Muslims worldwide
  • Meaning: "Submission to the will of Allah"
  • Holy Book: The Quran (preserved in original Arabic)
  • Final Prophet: Muhammad (peace be upon him)
  • Place of Worship: Mosque (Masjid)
  • Core Belief: Tawheed (Oneness of Allah)

Core Islamic Beliefs (Articles of Faith)

Islam is built upon six fundamental beliefs that every Muslim must accept.

1. Belief in Allah

Tawheed: Belief in the absolute Oneness of Allah. He has no partners, no children, no equals. He is the Creator, Sustainer, and Only One worthy of worship.

2. Belief in Angels

Angels are created from light who constantly worship Allah and carry out His commands. They include Jibreel (Gabriel), Mikael, Israfil, and the recording angels.

3. Belief in Divine Books

Allah revealed scriptures including the Torah (Tawrat), Psalms (Zabur), Gospel (Injeel), and finally the Quran - the only perfectly preserved scripture.

4. Belief in Prophets

Allah sent prophets to guide humanity, beginning with Adam and ending with Muhammad (peace be upon them all). All taught the same message of Tawheed.

5. Belief in Day of Judgment

This world will end, all beings will be resurrected, and judged according to their beliefs and deeds. Eternal paradise or hell awaits based on this judgment.

6. Belief in Divine Decree

Allah knows everything that will happen, and everything occurs by His will. However, humans have free will and are accountable for their choices.

The Five Pillars of Islam

These are the foundation of Muslim life and practice.

1

Shahada

Declaration of Faith
"I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His messenger." This is the entry into Islam.

2

Salah

Prayer
Five daily prayers facing the Kaaba in Mecca. Prayers are at dawn, midday, afternoon, sunset, and night. They maintain connection with Allah.

3

Zakat

Charity
Obligatory charity (2.5% of savings annually) for those who qualify. Purifies wealth and helps the poor, creating social justice.

4

Sawm

Fasting
Fasting during Ramadan from dawn to sunset. Develops self-discipline, God-consciousness, and empathy for the poor.

5

Hajj

Pilgrimage
Pilgrimage to Mecca once in a lifetime for those physically and financially able. Demonstrates unity of Muslims worldwide.

+

Ihsan

Excellence in Worship
To worship Allah as if you see Him, and though you don't see Him, He sees you. This is the highest level of faith.

Common Questions About Islam

Answers to frequently asked questions about Islam.

What do Muslims think about Jesus?

Muslims revere Jesus (Isa in Arabic) as one of the greatest prophets of Allah. He was born miraculously to the Virgin Mary, performed miracles by Allah's permission, and will return before the Day of Judgment. However, Muslims believe he was a human prophet, not divine, and not the son of God.

Is Islam compatible with science?

Yes. The Quran encourages observation, reflection, and seeking knowledge. Many verses discuss natural phenomena that align with modern science. Historically, Muslims made significant contributions to mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and other sciences during the Islamic Golden Age.

What does Islam say about other religions?

Islam teaches that Allah sent messengers to all nations. Muslims must believe in all prophets including those mentioned in the Bible. While Islam is considered the final and complete message, Muslims are commanded to respect people of other faiths and engage in dialogue with wisdom.

What is Jihad in Islam?

Jihad means "struggle" and has multiple meanings: the greater jihad is the struggle against one's own ego and sins; the lesser jihad is defending Muslims when attacked. It is not "holy war" but includes striving to improve oneself and society. Terrorism is strictly forbidden in Islam.
